# How to Cull Photos Faster in Lightroom (2026 Guide for Wedding Photographers) After a long shoot, the last thing you want is to sit in front of Lightroom for hours just deciding which photos to keep. But that’s exactly what happens. You come back with 2,000–3,000 photos, and suddenly you’re stuck in a loop: * zoom in * check sharpness * compare similar shots * look at eyes * go back * repeat After a while, it’s not even about quality anymore — you just want to finish. That’s not a skill problem. That’s **decision fatigue**. --- ## Quick Answer If you want to cull photos faster in Lightroom: - focus on the first pass only - avoid zooming into every image - use tools that surface sharpness and eye issues - stay inside Lightroom to avoid workflow friction The goal is not to pick final images — just eliminate weak ones quickly. --- ## Why Culling Feels So Draining Culling photos in Lightroom isn’t hard — it’s repetitive. For every photo, your brain is asking: * Is this sharp? * Are the eyes open? * Is this better than the previous one? Now multiply that by 2,000. That’s thousands of micro-decisions. At some point, your brain just slows down. You start: * keeping too many photos * missing small details * making inconsistent choices And what should take 1 hour turns into 3–5 hours. --- ## How long should culling take? For most wedding photographers: - 1,000 photos → ~45–60 minutes - 3,000 photos → 2–4 hours If it’s taking longer, the issue is usually not speed — it’s workflow. --- ## The Hidden Problem: Workflow Friction Most people think the issue is “too many photos”. But there’s another problem: > **breaking your workflow** A lot of tools require you to: * export photos * upload them * wait for processing * return to Lightroom Even if the AI is fast, the process itself slows you down. --- ## What is the fastest way to cull photos in Lightroom? The fastest Lightroom culling workflow focuses on reducing decisions: ### 1. Sharpness ranking Instead of zooming into every photo, you quickly see which ones are actually in focus. ### 2. Eye detection Closed or half-closed eyes are flagged automatically. ### 3. Grouping similar shots Burst shots are easier to compare. ### 4. Faster first pass You’re not deciding everything — just filtering out weak images. --- ## Local vs Cloud Culling One underrated factor: > **where the processing happens** Local tools: - no uploads - no waiting - work offline - faster feedback For large shoots, this makes a big difference. --- ## A Simpler Approach: Stay Inside Lightroom Some photographers are moving toward: > **culling faster without leaving Lightroom** Instead of switching tools, they: - stay in their workflow - reduce repetitive decisions - speed up the first pass --- ## A Tool That Helps With This If you want to try this workflow without doing everything manually: FireCull is a Lightroom plugin that: - ranks sharpness automatically - flags closed or occluded eyes - runs fully locally (no uploads) - works directly inside Lightroom You can test it on a real shoot in about 30–40 minutes. 👉 https://firecull.ai --- ## Final Thought Culling photos will probably never be fun.
